Stuff I've Made


Patient Passport



The idea for Patient Passport came to me after a discussion with a friend about the poor signage at the hospital at which she works. She indicated that she wasted about a quarter of most of her work days guiding patients to their appointments. Furthermore, she stated that the hospital administration often complained about losing money due to patients being late or missing their appointments entirely.
I decided to create an app for a theoretical hospital that would use pictures and text to guide patients to their appointments upon arrival at the hospital. I chose the MEAN stack in which to build the app. I built a database in Mongo, the backend in Node/Express and then used Angular to handle the rendering on the front end.


ReadMe



Built on Ruby on Rails, ReadMe is a full stack web application built in collaboration with fellow students from General Assembly's Web Development Immersive. ReadMe digitizes the experience of participating book clubs. The app allows for users to login (including an option to login with a Facebook account) to join book clubs, participate in discussions and share their favorite reads with other users.


Seattle Showdown



Seattle Showdown is a full stack web application that allows for Seattle-area concert goers to RSVP to shows, arrange ride-shares and share reviews. The backend was built using Express/Node.js and a PostgreSQL database. The app utilizes the Bandsintown API to provide the user with a robust search feature to find pertinent concert details for their favorite artists playing in and around Seattle.


Super Mario Bros: Lost in Capitol Hill



In this front-end application, our favorite, unlikely, plumber heroes find themselves lost in Seattle's Capitol Hill neighborhood. They must use their memories to find a series of hipster essentials in order to find their way out. The game logic was written in jQuery and heavily styled with CSS. The game board was modeled after the memory game in Super Mario Brothers 3 for the original 8-bit Nintendo.




About Me

I remember having a blast tinkering around with websites when I was in junior high school (think Geocities). I would lose myself in the design of my sites and would obsess over the smallest details. I taught myself a fair amount of HTML, and Javascript during this time. However, as I got older, I slowly disconnected from this passion, and thought that a career in law might be for me (as it was for every member of my immediate family). So, after completing my Bachelor's degree in Public Affairs at Indiana University, I spent the next eight years working in a variety of paralegal and investigator roles for various agencies and legal departments.

I eventually came to the conclusion that becoming an attorney was just not for me. I also found that my passion for technology had been reignited. These two realizations occurred at the perfect time, as I began researching the idea of attending a coding bootcamp. I chose the Web Development Immersive at General Assembly because of its stellar reputation, incredible outcomes statistics, and my gut feeling that it was the right fit for me. I put in my two week notice at work, and took the plunge. My experience was intense, informative, (sometimes) terrifying, rewarding, and most importantly it taught me how to think like a coder, think-through and solve problems, work effectively on a development team and "learn to learn".

When I'm not coding, you can find me trying to make people laugh, eating Mediterranean food, snapping pictures, exploring the world, or dancing akwardly at indie rock concerts.
