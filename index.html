Stuff I've Made

ReadMe



Built on Ruby on Rails, ReadMe is a full stack web application built in collaboration with other WDI students. ReadMe digitizes the experience of participating book clubs. The app allows for users to login (including an option to login with a Facebook account) to join book clubs, participate in discussions and share their favorite reads with other users.

Seattle Showdown



Seattle Showdown is a full stack web application that allows for Seattle-area concert goers to RSVP to shows, arrange ride-shares and share reviews. The backend was built using Express/Node.js and a PostgreSQL database. The app utilizes the Bandsintown API to provide the user with a robust search feature to find pertinent concert details for their favorite artists playing in and around Seattle.




About Me

After a short vacation, one day to be exact, my now wife and I decided that Seattle was the city for us. We saved some money, packed up our car and said our goodbyes. Then, we proceeded to drive 2,250 miles from Indianapolis, Indiana without job offers in hand. We knew that taking this risk was a necessary step toward our goal of living our lives exactly as we wanted. We have now spent two and half wonderful years in this city that we love.

I come from a legal background and have worked as a paralegal and investigator for both public defense agencies and corporate legal departments. Ultimately, I decided that the legal field was just not for me, so I again made a major life change and enrolled in the Web Development Immersive at General Assembly. It was a challenging, yet rewarding experience during which I learned some new skills and met some amazing people. Furthermore, I was able to channel my real passion for utilizing technology to help people solve problems and generally add value to their lives.

When I'm not coding, you can find me trying to make people laugh, eating Mediterranean food, snapping pictures, exploring the world, or dancing akwardly at indie rock concerts.
